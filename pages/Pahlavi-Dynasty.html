Pahlavi Dynasty

The Pahlavi dynasty (1925–1979 CE) marked Iran’s transition into the modern age. Founded by Reza Shah Pahlavi after the fall of the Qajar dynasty, this era was defined by modernization, centralization, and the creation of a national identity built on Persian heritage. Reza Shah transformed Iran’s infrastructure, reformed education, established the national railway, and replaced Persia’s name internationally with Iran. His son, Mohammad Reza Shah Pahlavi, continued modernization through the White Revolution, introducing land reforms, expanding education, and advancing women’s rights — while also aligning Iran with the West. Despite rapid progress, growing political unrest and social inequality culminated in the 1979 Revolution, ending over 2,500 years of monarchy and beginning the Islamic Republic.
